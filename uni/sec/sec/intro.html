2Basic definitions[up]

These definitions were written as summaries based on my understanding of the slides. When needed, the original notes should be consulted to check for misunderstandings.

Some definitions were lifted word-for-word due to difficulty in rephrasing while maintaining clarity.

Computer security

methods to keep computer systems and digital assets free from risk

Asset

something valuable

tangible like machinery, or

intangible like reputation

Threat

circumstance/event that puts assets at risk

Threat actors

that may or may not have malign intent

malign intent means it is an attack

otherwise, they may pose accidental risk

Vulnerability

a weakness in a component of a system—a potential attack surface that may be used as a point of entry

internet etc. are potential external attack surfaces

systems reachable from inside are also viable internal attack surfaces

humans are also an attack surface

Exploits

tools used to exploit vulnerabilities, usually reusable on many targets (economies of scale)

Risks

a risk needs a motivated and able threat, an exploitable vulnerability, and a negative/impact harm upon successful attack to manifest

2.1Desired properties[up]

Confidentiality

information not being disclosed to unauthorized entities

Integrity

assets should not be altered by unauthorized entities

Information integrity

unauthorized alteration should be detected and rejected

Availability

information and resources should be available to authorized parties

Authorization

determines which actors have the right to access which resources

Authentication

verification of identity of an actor

Non-repudiation

means an actor cannot deny having taken an action
